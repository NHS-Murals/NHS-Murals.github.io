Untitled (Don't take life too seriously)

Chelsea Gisolfi & James [indiscernible] (2010)

Located: 3rd Floor, E House, Room 306E

A mural on the door of room 306E. It is primarily composed of a quote surrounded by ladybugs: Don't take life too seriously you'll never get out alive. Alive is in red rather than the typical black text of the quote. Below this are headshots of Jake the Dog and Finn the Human from the cartoon Adventure Time, Jake appears to be drooling.

Notes: He's not credited here but the quote originates from one Elbert Hubbard[1] (1856-1915), an American editor and essayist[2]. Speaking of, the quote and the murals of Finn and Jake appear to have been painted separately by Chelsea Gisolfi & someone named James respectively. I cannot read James' last name (or even fully confirm if their name is James not Jones or something) because of the signature's degradation and font. If you know anything about this guy, please reach out.

Oh, and the posters aren't part of the mural by the way. I just can't take them off, sorry.
