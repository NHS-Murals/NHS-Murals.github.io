Untitled (I think, therefore I am)

Unknown

Located: 3rd Floor, E House-D House Atrium

A small rectangular mural depicting only the upper half pale-skinned face with particularly striking blue eyes, above those eyes, about where the forehead is located, is the quote: "I THINK THEREFORE I AM". The face is flanked on both sides by a busy bunch of colorful patterns that notably include swirls, arrows, and stripes to name a few.

Notes: This mural is above a display case on the wall, hence the size. The quote is a translation of French philosopher Ren√© Descartes' cogito, ergo sum which first appeared in his Discourse on Method published in 1637.[1]
